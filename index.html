About Me

I am a researcher in cancer biology, RNA therapeutics, genomics, and bioinformatics with a strong publication record in molecular biosciences. My work focuses on developing RNA-based therapeutic strategies for brain cancers and building computational pipelines for biomarker discovery and mRNA-based vaccine design.

Currently, I serve as a Postdoctoral Fellow at the University of Otago's Department of Biochemistry, where I lead projects on focused ultrasound-guided RNA therapeutics for glioblastoma and Parkinson's disease. I'm also building a computational vaccine design pipeline for pandemic preparedness through the NZ RNA Platform Grant.

My journey has taken me from biomedical sciences in Sheffield to molecular pathology in Leicester, and now to the cutting edge of RNA therapeutics in Dunedin. As a founding shareholder of Amaroq Therapeutics and consultant for RNAFold.AI, I bridge academia and biotechnology, translating discoveries into real-world applications.
